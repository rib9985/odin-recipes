Feijoada

It's time for one of the most famous and popular Brazilian dishes: Feijoada. AKA the Brazilian Black Bean Stew consists of black beans cooked and stewed with smoked pork, sausages, porchetta, and seasonings making for a scrumptious delicious meal. Take care with this dish, it's quite hard to not fall in love with the mouth-watering Feijoada!

The recipe will make a stew for 10 people.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dry black beans (soaked overnight)*
  • tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 ounces slab bacon (rind removed), diced
  • 1 pound pork ribs, cut into individual ribs
  • 2 Mexican chorizo sausages, sliced
  • 1 smoked sausage, such as linguica or kielbasa, sliced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 bay leaves
  • water

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl with water, soak beans overnight.
  2. When you are ready to make your stew, in a large heavy-bottom soup pot, over medium heat, add the oil and bacon. Cook until crisp and transfer to a plate.
  3. Use the same saucepan to brown ribs and sausages in batches. (You will want to be sure to cook the sliced Mexican chorizo on its own, as it can be very greasy. Drain the grease before continuing.) Set each aside as cooked.
  4. If needed, add more oil to the pan. On medium-high, saut√© onion and garlic until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add tomatoes and cook for another 3 minutes.
  5. Drain and rinse the soaked beans. Add them to the pot along with the ribs, bacon, sausages, salt, pepper, bay leave. Cover with water (about 8 cups).
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil and reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it cook for 2 to 2 and a half hours, or until the beans are sof.
  7. If the stew is too liquidy, uncover the saucepan and continue to cook for another 20 minutes to allow some of the liquid to evaporat.
  8. Serve with white rice and sprinkle some farofa on top.

Pairings

  • The classical dishes to serve with Feijoada are rice and manioc flour. Make sure to prepare them in advance!
  • Feijoada also pairs extremely well with one of our previous tasty recipes, the brazilian-style vinagrete.
  • Or how about grilling some BBQ-flavoured pork-chops? Also goes great with grilled bananas!
  • If you're up for a something to cleanse the palette, how about some sweet lemonade for that acidic, juicy break?
  • But if you really want to go all out, go for a cold beer!
